Move from a vague idea to an actionable goal.

What do you want to achieve?

Start with the big picture. Describe the goal or result you have in mind. Don't worry about being perfect; just get the initial thought down.

Stuck? Get a tip.

Trick: Start with the Opposite

If you're blocked, try defining what you *don't* want. "I don't want to feel stressed about money." The opposite of that (e.g., "I want to achieve financial peace") can be a powerful starting point for a goal.

Why is this important to you?

Understanding your core motivation is key. This 'why' will fuel your efforts when things get tough. What's the real value behind this for you?

Stuck? Get a tip.

Trick: Use the "5 Whys"

If your 'why' feels superficial, dig deeper. Ask "why?" to your own answer, five times. Example: "I want more money." (1. Why?) "To feel secure." (2. Why?) "So I'm not worried about the future." ... This uncovers the true emotional core of your goal.

Let's get specific.

A vague goal is hard to act on. "Success" needs a clear definition. Answering these smaller questions helps build the bigger picture.

Stuck? Get a tip.

Trick: Who, What, Where?

Break it down with simple questions:
• Who is involved? (e.g., me, a partner, my target customers)
• What exactly am I creating/doing? (e.g., a product, a service, a skill)
• Where will this happen? (e.g., online, in a specific city)

How will you measure progress?

How will you know, without a doubt, that you've achieved your goal? Define clear, numeric milestones or key performance indicators (KPIs).

Stuck? Get a tip.

Trick: Leading vs. Lagging Indicators

Think of two types of metrics:
• Lagging: The final result (e.g., "$10k in revenue"). You can only see this after the fact.
• Leading: The daily actions that get you there (e.g., "make 5 sales calls per day"). You can control this.
Defining both gives you a result to aim for and actions to take.

When do you want to achieve this?

A goal without a deadline is just a dream. Setting a realistic target date creates a sense of urgency and is crucial for planning your next steps.

Stuck? Get a tip.

Trick: Work Backwards

If a big deadline feels overwhelming, break it down. If your goal is for one year from now, what must be true in 6 months for you to be on track? What about 1 month? What about next week? Focus only on the immediate, smaller deadline.
